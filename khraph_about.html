About

KHEPERAH


RAY


Whether I'm taking something apart to learn about its internal architecture or tinkering with a device in my free time, my mind revolves around our the everyday use and utility of technology in life. I have always been drawn to design, and through my curiosity about the internal mechanics of things I have developed a true passion to create new technology for the benefit of others.

One medium that has helped me in my creative process is photography. Being behind the lens has provided me an understanding of perspective and how it shapes our conception of both the world and what is possible within it. By working through perspective as Iâ€™ve been taking photos I have learned how to change my frame of mind often and work beyond boundaries to create innovative and aesthetic works of art and design.

My inspiration and art has through a number of ways, impacted my view of technology. I have seen that tech can be used as a means of communication, entertainment, and growth, and my dream is to be an innovator producing compelling technology that helps people in life by understanding (and building products around) their unique perspective.

In pursuit of my passion I have decided to major in Electrical Engineering at the University of San Diego with a minor in Computer Science. I am heavily invested in my classes and extracurricular activities on campus. Being the Secretary of the National Society of Black Engineers and a valued voice in the Student Alumni Association, has given me many invaluable lessons on time management, and executive planning that has truly helped me in my freelance professional work.

At the moment most of my time is spent on my class work, student organizations and freelance work in photography, graphic design, and web development. After graduating in 2018, I plan to start my own company centered around the cultivation of technology in the black community. Some of my inspiration arises from the Little Black Pearl center in the Kenwood neighborhood of Chicago as well as organizations like BlackGirlsCode and Black Tech Mecca.

Most of the focus of my adult life will be toward educating the youth through technology and providing them the space and resources to be as creative as they dream to be. I believe that using technology will allow us to envision a better future for all people and I hope my work helps start a movement that makes all people more aware of the power of technology, design, and art to transform our world.
